Easy Pay International
Making Transactions Easy

EasyPay International is a new, innovative, user-friendly mobile platform that facilitates peer-to-peer, online, and in-store transactions keeping the cost of international transactions at its minimum with superior help center available 24/7 in most languages and keeping security measures as a top priority.

After the competitive analysis, SWOT, UX analysis was done, and interviews and surveys took place, my initial hypothesis emerged:

The user needs a convenient and secure way to perform multi-currency transactions when traveling abroad, to transfer money to a friend, and to shop online on international websites in a safe, secure, and reliable way.

The target audience is identified as tech savvy, frequent travelers either for leisure or work, people with families and friends in another country, international online shoppers, generation X, millennial, and generation Z - people from 18 - 45 years old.

USABILITY TESTING

Now that my first high fidelity prototypes are ready, I started planning usability testing.

I wrote a test plan to identify the goal of the usability test and its objectives. I recruited 6 participants, and scheduled sessions with them. Then, I created a test script with 3 tasks to complete, which helped me to ensure the flow of my sessions and predict any difficulties.

AFTER USABILITY TESTING

Tasting and iteration phases has enabled me to gather feedback from the users and incorporate their suggestions into designing new features and enhancing existing ones.

I grouped the issues in rainbow spreadsheet to get a better picture of problems and their severity. I identified 5 issues and possible solutions to address them.

Issue 1

No sufficient confirmation after sending money.

Evidence

2 out of 6 participants expressed concerns about this issue.

Suggested Change

Adding a confirmation page after sending money.

Issue 2

No sufficient confirmation after requesting money.

Evidence

4 out of 6 participants seemed surprised that there is no confirmation after requesting money.

Suggested Change

Adding a confirmation page after making a request.

Issue 3

The latest transactions on homepage didn't reflect the send / request money transaction.

Evidence

4 out of 6 participants asked about latest transaction being updated after send/request transaction.

Suggested Change

Adding highlighted latest transaction.

Issue 4

There was no way to delete a method of payment.

Evidence

1 participant asked where would he click if he wanted to delete a card that is listed.

Suggested Change

Adding a “delete” sign next to a card that is being listed.

GESTALT PRINCIPLES IN ACTION

Visual Hierarchy

The user can see right away what is the most important on the screen, prioritizing the balance, and the latest transactions.

Law of Similarity

It is implemented through consistent use of color, font, and iconography across the screen. This creates visual cohesion and aids in grouping related elements together, enhancing user comprehension.

Emphasis

It is strategically applied to highlight elements that require faster recognition, such as send and request money buttons in the menu, balance and the latest transactions. These elements stand out prominently, facilitating quicker user engagement to accomplish a desired task.

HOME SCREEN

Law of Similarity

The consistent use of color, font, and iconography across the screen make it pleasant and clear for a user.

Emphasis

The emphasis is put into sign in buttons through darker shade of color to make it clear for users how to enter the application.

Proportion

The size of the buttons make it easy to find the way the user wants to sign in or sign up to EasyPay International app.

Unity and Balance

The screen is well balanced by lifting the logo a bit higher. An alignment of buttons makes the page visually better organized.
